...WINTER WEATHER ADVISORY IN EFFECT FROM 1 AM TO 9 AM EST

...WIND CHILL ADVISORY REMAINS IN EFFECT FROM 3 AM TO 9 AM EST
MONDAY...

* WHAT...Light freezing rain and freezing drizzle expected. Total
  accumulations of a trace to a few hundredths of an inch are 
  expected. Also, very cold wind chills will occur. Expect wind 
  chill values of 10 to 15 degrees above zero.

* WHERE...Charleston and Tidal Berkeley counties.

* WHEN...From 1 AM to 9 AM EST Monday for the Winter Weather
  Advisory and 3 AM to 9 AM EST Monday for the Wind Chill
  Advisory.

* ADDITIONAL DETAILS...Most ice accumulations will occur on
  elevated surfaces such as bridges, overpasses, trees and power
  lines. Temperatures will drop into the mid to upper 20s. Winds 
  will be northeast 10 to 15 mph with gusts to 25 mph.

PRECAUTIONARY/PREPAREDNESS ACTIONS...

A Winter Weather Advisory means that periods of freezing rain
will cause travel difficulties. Be prepared for slippery roads.
Slow down and use caution while driving. The latest road
conditions for the state you are calling from can be obtained by
calling 5 1 1.

A Wind Chill Advisory means that cold air and the wind will
combine to create low wind chills. Frost bite and hypothermia can
occur if precautions are not taken. Make sure you wear a hat and
gloves.
